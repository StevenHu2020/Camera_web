—— Do you really need high pixel density?

4k
1080P

While it's not necessary to relentlessly pursue high resolution when purchasing a camera, it's essential to have a basic understanding: the device's maximum shooting resolution should ideally exceed the desired output video resolution. Opting for a resolution one step higher allows for better detail retention after compression, but going significantly beyond your intended output resolution offers little practical benefit in video production.

For instance, if you intend to create scenic videos in 4K resolution, your camera should ideally support shooting in 6K or higher. On the other hand, if your goal is to produce 1080p vlogs documenting your daily life, selecting a device capable of 4K video recording is typically sufficient.

It's important to note that excessively high resolutions also demand more from your video editing and playback equipment. If you don't require top-tier video quality, it's advisable to avoid unnecessary expenses.
